Age vs. Blood Pressure

People with diabetes are more likely to also have high blood pressure or hypertension. The chance of having high blood pressure increases as you get older. High blood pressure can increase the risk of diabetes complications such as diabetic eye and kidney problems. Managing blood pressure will be a part of a person's overall diabetes care plan.

BMI Categories:
Obesity = BMI of 30 or greater
Overweight = 25–29.9
Normal weight = 18.5–24.9
Underweight = <18.5

Your Body Mass Index (BMI) can have an incremental impact on the risk of developing Type II Diabetes. Being overweight is classified with an index between 25 and 29.9 and higher classes go up in 5 index increments all the way up to 40 which is Obesity Class III. Type II Diabetes risk may be incrementally higher in those with a higher body mass index. It is important to understand the risk factors of managing your BMI to shorten the time to diagnose and treat Type II Diabetes.
